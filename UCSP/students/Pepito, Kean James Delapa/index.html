Politics

The activities associated with the governance of a country or other area especially the debate or conflict among individuals of parties having or hoping to achieve power.


Society

A large group of people who live together in an organized way, making decisions about how to do things and sharing the work that needs to be done.


Culture

Refers to the cumulative deposit of knowledge, experience, beliefs, values, attitudes, meanings, hierarchies, religion, notion of time, roles, spatial relations, concepts of the universe, and material objects and possessions acquired by a group of people in the course of generations through individual and group striving.


Nationality

It is a legal affiliation with a specific country, determining rights and obligations. It's often used interchangeably with citizenship but may have subtle differences.


Ethnicity

Refers to cultural and social charactersitics shared by a group, often by heritage, language or traditions.


Discrimination

It is an unjust or prejudicial treatment of groups based on characteristics such as race, gender, religion, or other factors, leading to unequal treatment or opportunities.


Ostracism

It is the exclusion or rejection of individuals from a group or society, often leading to their isolation or exclusion.


Sex

Refers to the biological distinction between male and female based on physical and genetic characteristics.


Gender

Refers to the social and cultural roles, behaviours and expectation associated with being male, female, or none-binary, distinct from biological sex.


Enthocentrism

Promotes individual's culture as "the most efficient and superior".

A belief that one's culture is better than the culture of others. It creates a superiority complex that is more often than not a flash point of abuse and conflict.


Relativism

It is the belief that truth, morality, and knowledge are not absolute but dependet on cultural, societal, or individual perspectives and contexts.
