Human Evolution




image taken: The Washington Post

Human evolution is the lengthy process of change by which people originated from apelike ancestors. Scientific evidence shows that the physical and behavioral traits shared by all people originated from apelike ancestors and evolved over a period of approximately six million years.

Process of evolution

Evolution is a natural process that leads to the emergence, adaptation, and extinction of species. All species, including humans, have evolved through the inheritance of genetic material. In sexually reproducing animals, a species comprises individuals capable of interbreeding and producing fertile offspring. Each species is given a unique scientific name, such as "Homo sapiens" for modern humans. Evolution involves changes in the genetic material, DNA, inherited from parents, particularly in the proportions of genes within a population. Genes determine protein production, and mutations can alter the genetic information. These genetic changes influence an organism's development and behavior, affecting its survival and reproduction.



image taken: source

Evolution doesn't change individual organisms but rather the inherited traits within a population. Adaptive genetic changes are passed from parents to offspring, becoming common in the population. This enhances the chances of survival and reproduction, but environmental changes can disrupt this. Over time, genetic changes can transform a species' way of life, including diet, growth, and habitat. Human evolution occurred through genetic variations favoring adaptability to environmental shifts, altering our way of life.
