HUMAN BEHAVIOR

Human behavior is the potential and expressed capacity (mentally, physically, and socially) of human individuals or groups to respond to internal and external stimuli throughout their life. Behavior is driven by genetic and environmental factors that affect an individual. Human behaviour refers to the way humans act and interact. It is based on and influenced by several factors, such as genetic make-up, culture and individual values and attitudes. Types of human behavior include moral versus molecular, overt versus covert, voluntary versus involuntary, and conscious versus unconscious. Examples of human behavior include conflict, communication, cooperation, creativity, play, social interaction, tradition, and work. Human behavior has revealed that 90% of the population can be classified into four basic personality types: Optimistic, Pessimistic, Trusting and Envious.
First and foremost, studying human behavior helps us comprehend ourselves better. It allows us to gain insight into our own actions, thoughts, and emotions. By observing and analyzing human behavior, we can identify patterns and trends that shape our lives, thus enabling us to make more informed choices and decisions. Strongly rooted in psychology and sociology, studies of human behavior give us an academic understanding of motivations, productivity, and how teams work. In turn, these insights can help make workplaces or any group setting more productive. Behaviors play key roles in survival, long- and short-term health, and emotional and physical well-being. Some behaviors are instinctual, and others are conscious choices. Behaviors result from a complex interaction between genetics and the environment, and they include emotional and physical actions and reactions.
Certainly, here are some examples of human behavior:
1. Smiling - People often smile to express happiness, friendliness, or to show they are pleased with something.
2. Holding the Door - Holding the door for someone is a common act of politeness and courtesy.
3. Aggression - Human behavior can also include aggression, such as yelling, physical altercations, or aggressive driving.
4. Sharing on Social Media - Posting photos, opinions, and updates on social media platforms is a behavior shaped by the digital age.
5. Exercise - Engaging in physical activities like running, cycling, or going to the gym is a behavior aimed at maintaining physical health.
6. Lying - People may lie to avoid consequences, protect someone's feelings, or for personal gain.
7. Shopping - Consumer behavior, such as shopping for goods and services, is influenced by personal preferences, needs, and societal trends.
8. Reading a Book - Reading is a common behavior for gaining knowledge, entertainment, or relaxation.
9. Helping Others - Altruistic behavior, like helping a stranger in need, reflects a desire to assist and make a positive impact.
10. Waving Hello and Goodbye - A common social behavior to greet and bid farewell to others.
A few examples of human behavior can be complex and influenced by various factors such as culture, upbringing, emotions, and societal norms.
