WHAT IS THE POLITICS, SOCIETY, AND CULTURE?

POLITICS:

Politics refers to the activities, actions, and policies used to gain and hold power in a government or to influence the government. It encompasses the process by which individuals and groups make decisions that affect society, often involving the allocation of resources, the creation of laws, and the implementation of policies. Politics can occur at various levels, from local government and national governance to international relations.

SOCIETY:

Society refers to a group of individuals living together in an organized community. It encompasses the relationships, interactions, and structures that govern human behavior within a specific group. Societies can vary widely in terms of culture, norms, values, and institutions, influencing the lives of their members.

CULTURE:

Culture is a complex and multifaceted term that can be defined in various ways depending on the context. Generally, culture refers to the shared beliefs, values, customs, behaviors, symbols, language, and practices that characterize a particular group of people, such as a community, society, or nation. It encompasses the social norms, traditions, rituals, art, music, cuisine, clothing, and other aspects of life that are passed down from generation to generation within a specific group.

ETHNOCENTRISM VS CULTURAL RELATIVISM

ETHNOCENTRISM:

Ethnocentrism is a concept in anthropology and sociology that refers to the tendency of individuals or groups to evaluate and judge other cultures or ethnic groups based on the standards, values, and beliefs of their own culture. It involves the belief that one's own culture or ethnic group is superior to others, leading to a biased and often negative view of unfamiliar or different cultures.

CULTURAL RELATIVISM:

Cultural relativism is the view that ethical and social standards reflect the cultural context from which they are derived. Cultural relativists uphold that cultures differ fundamentally from one another, and so do the moral frameworks that structure relations within different societies.
