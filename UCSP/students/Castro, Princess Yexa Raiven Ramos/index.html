Learnings

In order to deal with the human and social variation and differences in our world, we should take a cultural relativism approach. This approach recognizes that, while humans all belong to the same species, they practice different sets of behaviours that come from the knowledge systems they adhere to. These knowledge systems are affected by the environment, technology and global movement, so it is important to take into account the different cultural contexts that come into play. By adopting this approach, we can gain a better understanding of the diversity of human experience and be more respectful of our differences.

Discrimination based on race and ethnicity is a complex issue that has a long history. Discrimination against people based on their racial or ethnic identity can take many forms, including unequal access to education, housing, employment, and health care; biased criminal justice decisions; and unequal treatment in other areas of life. In many countries, laws have been passed to protect people from discrimination based on their race or ethnicity, but these laws do not always result in real change. To truly address discrimination based on race and ethnicity, there must be a focus on creating an equitable society that grants everyone equal rights and opportunities regardless of their background. This means making sure that people of different racial and ethnic backgrounds are represented in leadership positions, instilling cultural sensitivity in the workplace, and challenging stereotypes and unconscious biases. It also involves advocating for policies that will create a more inclusive society and ensure that all people are treated with respect and dignity.
